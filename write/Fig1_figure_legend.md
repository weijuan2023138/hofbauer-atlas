# Figure 1. A single-cell transcriptomic atlas of Hofbauer cell development across human gestation.

**(A)** Schematic overview of the study design and data integration strategy. Eight independent scRNA-seq datasets spanning first (GW4.5–10), second (GW11–24), and third (GW32–38) trimesters were integrated after quality control, yielding 17,896 Hofbauer cells for downstream analysis.

**(B)** UMAP visualization of 17,896 Hofbauer cells colored by six transcriptionally defined subtypes: Pro-inflammatory (IL1B, CCL3, CXCL8), MHCII+ Antigen-presenting (HLA-DRA, HLA-DPA1, CD74), Homeostatic (FOLR2, CD163, LYVE1, DAB2), PRKN+ Autophagy (PRKN, SQSTM1, MAP1LC3B), Vascular remodeling (SPP1, FN1, VEGFA), and MKI67+ Proliferating (MKI67, TOP2A, BIRC5). Subtypes were identified by Louvain clustering (resolution = 0.15) after Harmony batch correction.

**(C)** UMAP split by trimester, showing the systematic shift of Hofbauer cell transcriptomic states along gestational age: first-trimester cells concentrate in the left region (pro-inflammatory/proliferative), second-trimester cells expand toward the center (homeostatic), and third-trimester cells migrate rightward (antigen-presenting/vascular remodeling).

**(D)** Proportions of the six Hofbauer subtypes across trimesters, displayed as line plots. The Pro-inflammatory subtype declines from 42.1% (first trimester) to 7.3% (third trimester), while the Homeostatic subtype expands from 8.4% to 35.7%, and the MHCII+ Antigen-presenting subtype increases from 3.2% to 28.9%.

**(E)** Gene set enrichment analysis (GSEA) of third-trimester versus first-trimester Hofbauer cells using the Hallmark gene set collection. First-trimester-enriched pathways include E2F targets, MYC targets, and oxidative phosphorylation; third-trimester-enriched pathways feature NF-κB-mediated TNFα signaling, complement cascade, interferon-γ response, and IL6-JAK-STAT3 signaling. NES, normalized enrichment score; FDR, false discovery rate.

**(F)** Module scores for five functional programs (Progenitor, Proliferation, Metabolism, Remodeling, Immune) across trimesters. Box plots show median and interquartile range. The Progenitor module declines monotonically (median 0.42 to −0.31), the Immune module shows the largest monotonic increase (−0.48 to 0.56), and the Remodeling module peaks at mid-gestation.

**(G)** Dot plot of 20 representative genes organized into three functional tiers: developmental regulation (TREM2, CEBPA, SOX4, BHLHE41), tissue remodeling (SPP1, FN1, TIMP1, MMP14), and immune effector function (FCGR3A, C1QA, C1QB, C1QC, CXCL8). Dot size represents the fraction of expressing cells; color intensity represents mean expression level. C1q complement components show >100-fold upregulation from first to third trimester.

**Supplementary Figure 1A.** UMAP visualization of all placental cell types with classifier-based annotation, confirming that Hofbauer cells (FOLR2, CD163, CD68, CSF1R) form a distinct, well-separated cluster from fibroblasts, endothelial cells, trophoblasts, and other immune populations.

**Supplementary Figure 1B.** Dataset composition overview showing the distribution of samples by disease condition (normal, preeclampsia, miscarriage, infection) and gestational age, forming a complementary matrix for multi-dimensional phenotypic anchoring.

**Supplementary Figure 1C.** Dot plot of canonical marker genes (FOLR2, CD163, CD68, HLA-DRA, SPP1, FN1, MKI67, PRKN, etc.) across all six Hofbauer subtypes, confirming the transcriptional identity and specificity of each subtype.

**Supplementary Figure 1D.** Additional dot plot showing extended marker gene panel for Hofbauer subtypes.

**Supplementary Figure 1X.** Protein-level validation of eight Hofbauer signature proteins (FOLR2, CD163, MRC1, CD68, FCGR3A, HLA-DRA, TREM2, C1QA) by immunofluorescence and flow cytometry, confirming concordance with transcript-level expression patterns.
