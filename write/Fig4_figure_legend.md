# Figure 4. CellChat analysis decodes the Hofbauer cell-centered ligand-receptor communication network in the placental stroma, validated by spatial transcriptomics.

**(A)** Bubble plot showing the global incoming signaling landscape received by Hofbauer cells (HB) from seven placental cell types (FB, vEC, fEC, SCT, VCT, dNK, CD14_M). Bubble size and color intensity represent communication probability; rows are grouped by signaling pathway. ECM/matrix pathways (COLLAGEN, FN1, LAMININ, SPP1, THBS) dominate the incoming signal repertoire.

**(B)** Bubble plot of outgoing signaling from HB to the same seven target cell types. The target cell ranking (vEC > CD14_M > dNK > fEC > FB > VCT > SCT) mirrors the incoming source ranking, indicating bidirectional communication symmetry. SPP1 and FN1 pathways dominate both incoming and outgoing directions.

**(C)** Bar plot quantifying the number of significant ligand-receptor (L-R) pairs for incoming and outgoing communication between HB and each partner cell type. HB functions as a net immune signal exporter: the outgoing/incoming ratio is 1.57 for CD14_M (22 vs. 14 pairs) and 1.75 for dNK (21 vs. 12 pairs).

**(D)** Chord diagram of the FB → HB communication axis at the gene-pair level. Collagen pathways (COL1A1, COL1A2, COL4A1, COL4A2, COL5A1, COL6A1, COL6A2, COL6A3) with integrin receptors (ITGA1, ITGB1 heterodimers) constitute the dominant signaling mode. FN1-ITGAV/ITGB1 is the second strongest pair (probability 0.00068). TGF-β superfamily ligands (TGFB1, BMP2/4/6, GDF5/6/7) from FB contribute a low-probability, high-diversity signaling strategy.

**(E)** Chord diagram of the vEC → HB communication axis. With 28 significant L-R pairs, this represents the densest single cell-to-cell dialogue in the network. ECM pathways (COLLAGEN, FN1) are shared with the FB axis, but vEC uniquely expresses PTPRM, mediating homophilic (PTPRM-PTPRM) cell-contact adhesion between endothelial cells and HB.

**(F)** Dot plot of the top 50 L-R gene pairs with Hofbauer cells, organized by incoming (FB/vEC/fEC → HB, top) and outgoing (HB → vEC/CD14_M/dNK, bottom) directions. Dot size represents communication probability; fill color indicates pathway category. SPP1 is the most prominent bidirectional signal (6 outgoing isoforms, 3 incoming isoforms), followed by FN1 (6 isoforms each direction).

**(G)** Spatial validation of four representative L-R pairs (SPP1→ITGAV, FN1→ITGB1, COL1A2→ITGA1, PTPRM↔PTPRM) in three mid-gestation placental samples (004, 014, 015) using Stereo-seq. For each sample and L-R pair, ligand-expressing cells are shown in red and receptor-expressing cells in blue. The leftmost column shows the cell-type spatial map (HB red, FB orange, fVEC blue) for anatomical reference. All four L-R pairs exhibit spatially overlapping expression patterns, confirming physical co-localization in tissue.

**(H)** Quantification of neighborhood co-localization enrichment for each L-R pair across the three samples. Bar plot shows mean enrichment (observed/random ratio, ±SD) within a 50-μm radius. All four pairs show significant enrichment above random expectation (p < 0.01, 100 spatial permutations). SPP1→ITGAV shows the strongest enrichment (3.46 ± 0.70), followed by FN1→ITGB1 (2.65 ± 0.46), COL1A2→ITGA1 (2.23 ± 0.36), and PTPRM↔PTPRM (1.43 ± 0.17). The horizontal dashed line at 1.0 indicates random expectation. **p < 0.01 (≥2 of 3 samples individually significant). Sample-level data: n = 3 biological replicates per pair.

**Supplementary Figure 4B.** Functional category summary of all significant L-R pairs, grouped by pathway category, showing that ECM/Matrix contributes the largest fraction of incoming signals to HB.

**Supplementary Figure 4C.** Chord diagrams for the three major HB outgoing communication axes: HB → vEC, HB → CD14_M, and HB → dNK. SPP1 and FN1 serve as the shared backbone pathways across all three target cell types.
