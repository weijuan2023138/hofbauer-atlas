# Figure 2. Transcription factor programming and chromatin remodeling drive the developmental transition of Hofbauer cells.

**(A)** Dot plot of 47 significantly differentially expressed transcription factors (TFs) between third-trimester and first-trimester Hofbauer cells (|log2FC| > 0.5, FDR < 0.01). Twenty-two TFs are enriched in first trimester (including CEBPA, MAFB, ID2, SOX4), and 25 TFs are enriched in third trimester (including STAT1, STAT3, NFKB1, RELB, NR4A3). STAT1 and STAT3 show the largest upregulation (log2FC = 2.84 and 2.17, respectively), while CEBPA shows prominent downregulation (log2FC = −1.93).

**(B)** Volcano plot of ATAC-seq differential accessibility peaks between term (GW32–38) and mid-gestation (GW11–24) FACS-purified Hofbauer cells. Red points, term-gained peaks (373 peaks, FDR < 0.05, |log2FC| > 1); blue points, mid-gestation-gained peaks (31 peaks). Term-specific open peaks show ~12-fold greater total signal intensity than mid-gestation-specific peaks, indicating asymmetric chromatin remodeling favoring regulatory element gain.

**(C)** Motif enrichment analysis of term-gained ATAC-seq peaks. The top five enriched TF binding motifs (ranked by E-value) are STAT3 (E-value = 3.2 × 10⁻¹², 7.8% of term peaks), STAT1 (5.7 × 10⁻¹⁰), RELB (8.1 × 10⁻⁹), NFKB2 (2.3 × 10⁻⁸), and KLF4 (1.6 × 10⁻⁷). The motif enrichment profile is concordant with TF expression changes in panel (A).

**(D)** Scatter plot integrating ATAC-seq chromatin accessibility (x-axis, log2 fold change of peak signal) with RNA-seq gene expression (y-axis, log2 fold change) for 2,141 genes with both data types. Of these, 1,969 genes (91.9%) show concordant direction of change (Cohen's κ = 0.84, p < 2.2 × 10⁻¹⁶). Key immune effector genes (C1QA, FCGR3A) are highlighted.

**(E)** GSEA of the 1,969 chromatin-transcription concordant genes using the Hallmark gene set collection. Downregulated programs include cell cycle (spindle assembly, G2M checkpoint, FDR < 10⁻²⁰) and mitochondrial translation (FDR < 10⁻¹⁵). Upregulated programs center on adaptive immunity: antigen processing and presentation (MHC class I/II, FDR = 6.3 × 10⁻¹⁸), complement activation (FDR = 2.1 × 10⁻¹⁴), and Fcγ receptor-mediated phagocytosis (FDR = 8.9 × 10⁻¹²).

**(F)** Heatmap of 45 representative genes organized into four functional modules: Development (CEBPA, MAFB, SQSTM1), Metabolism, Tissue Remodeling, and Immunity (C1QA/B/C, FCGR3A). Columns represent individual samples ordered by gestational age. Right panels show module score box plots per trimester, with the Immune module showing the largest increase (Δ = 1.09 standard units) and the strongest anticorrelation with Development and Metabolism modules (Pearson r = −0.87).

**(G)** Summary panel synthesizing the TF-chromatin-transcription regulatory axis: the CEBPA/MAFB/ID2 progenitor TF program is replaced by the STAT1/STAT3/NFKB immune TF program; STAT3 and STAT1 act as pioneer factors to open chromatin at immune effector gene loci; chromatin remodeling is broadly coupled to transcriptional activation (92% concordance); and the functional transition follows a sequential hierarchy of metabolism → remodeling → immunity.
