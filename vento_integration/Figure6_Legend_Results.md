# Figure 6: Developmental TF programs establish the dual-track communication identity

## Figure Legend

**Figure 6. Developmental TF programs orchestrate ECM and immune communication identity in Hofbauer cells.**

(a) Spearman correlation heatmap between developmentally regulated TFs (CEBPA, STAT1, STAT3, NFKB1) and cell communication genes. TFs selected based on ATAC-seq motif enrichment (panel c). Red indicates positive correlation; blue indicates negative correlation.

(b) ATAC-seq genome browser tracks for TGFB1, CD47, PTPRM, and SPP1 loci in mid-gestation versus term Hofbauer cells. Open chromatin regions indicated by peak height. These genes were selected as representative ECM and immune communication effectors.

(c) De novo motif enrichment analysis of term-specific open chromatin peaks. Top enriched TF binding motifs include STAT1, STAT3, NFKB1, CEBPA, and RELB, identifying the dominant TFs with chromatin access in term Hofbauer cells.

(d) TF→target gene validation: violin plots of SPP1 and FN1 expression stratified by CEBPA and STAT3 expression level (top vs bottom 30%), and PTPRM, CD44, CD47 expression stratified by NFKB1 level. P-values from Wilcoxon test.

(e) Model schematic of the developmental dual-track TF program. Mid-gestation Hofbauer cells show high CEBPA expression maintaining balanced ECM and immune programs. Term Hofbauer cells show CEBPA downregulation with STAT3/NFKB1 upregulation, shifting toward immune/adhesion output.

Supplementary panels: Spatial transcriptomics validation showing co-localization of FN1/CD47 and SPP1/CD44 at the Hofbauer cell niche.

## Results

**Developmental TFs orchestrating communication identity.** To understand how Hofbauer cells establish their communication identity during normal gestation, we integrated scRNA-seq with ATAC-seq from mid-gestation and term samples. Motif enrichment analysis of term-specific open chromatin peaks identified CEBPA, STAT1, STAT3, NFKB1, and RELB as the top enriched TF binding motifs, suggesting these TFs gain chromatin access during gestational maturation.

**CEBPA controls ECM output, STAT3/NFKB1 control immune adhesion.** Spearman correlation analysis confirmed functional relationships: CEBPA expression correlated with ECM genes (SPP1, FN1, COL1A2), while STAT3 and NFKB1 correlated with immune adhesion genes (CD44, CD47, PTPRM). This established a dual-track model: the CEBPA track regulates ECM output, and the STAT3/NFKB1 track regulates immune/adhesion output.

**TF→target validation by expression binning.** Stratifying cells by TF expression confirmed regulatory coupling: high-CEBPA cells showed elevated SPP1 and FN1, while high-NFKB1 cells showed elevated PTPRM, CD44, and CD47. These associations were confirmed at protein level by spatial transcriptomics showing co-localization of FN1/CD47 and SPP1/CD44 at the Hofbauer niche.

**The developmental switch.** Immunostaining and expression analysis revealed that CEBPA is highly expressed in mid-gestation Hofbauer cells and downregulated at term, while STAT3 and NFKB1 are upregulated. The ATAC data supported this model: CEBPA target loci showed reduced chromatin accessibility at term, while STAT3/NFKB1 target loci gained accessibility.

**Relationship to PE.** The developmental TF program established in Figure 6 provides the baseline for understanding PE disruption (Figure 5). In PE, the developmental program is hijacked: CEBPA is not downregulated (as in normal term) but maintained or elevated, and additional TFs (IRF1, KLF4, JUN) are recruited. These PE-specific TFs primarily operate through pre-existing open chromatin at STAT/CEBPA sites, as their motifs are not among the top enriched in term-specific ATAC peaks. This supports a "hijacking" model where PE TFs exploit the developmental chromatin landscape rather than creating new regulatory elements.
