## Results

Early and late PE disrupt the ECM/immune dual-track balance of Hofbauer cells through distinct TF programs:

**Dual-track framework globally activated in early PE (7a, 7f).** Early PE cells simultaneously occupied both ECM-high and immune-high regions of the transcriptional landscape, whereas late PE cells were confined to the immune-high axis. Normal late-pregnancy cells occupied an intermediate position between the two pathological states.

**Differential TF expression defines PE subtypes (7b).** IRF1 expression was elevated 2-fold in early PE relative to normal late pregnancy, while STAT3 was specifically elevated in late PE. CEBPA was modestly increased in early PE (FC=1.17). Both PE subtypes showed elevated STAT1 compared to normal.

**Modular output diverges between subtypes (7c).** Early PE showed significantly elevated scores for both ECM and immune modules. Late PE showed elevated immune module scores only, with ECM module scores comparable to normal late pregnancy.

**Regulatory coupling reveals expression-function discordance (7d).** KLF4 was the only TF maintaining robust coupling across all conditions (Spearman r>0.16 in Normal Late, PE, and Miscarriage). IRF1 showed coupling collapse specifically in PE (r=0.007), despite high expression, suggesting post-translational blockade. CEBPA showed weak or negative coupling across all groups, consistent with an indirect de-repression rather than direct activation mechanism.

**Subtype-level TF deployment (7e).** In early PE, CEBPA and IRF1 were uniformly elevated across all six Hofbauer subtypes. In late PE, STAT3 was selectively elevated in MHCII+ and Pro-inflammatory subtypes, while KLF4 showed the strongest coupling in Normal Late and late PE.

**Effector output converges to distinct pathogenic pathways (7g).** Early PE Hofbauer cells showed 3.8-fold higher FLT1 expression compared to normal late pregnancy, alongside elevated FN1, SPP1, and COL1A2. Late PE cells showed elevated HLA-DRA and CD74 (antigen presentation) and STAT3, with FLT1 levels comparable to normal.

**Conclusion.** Early PE is driven by CEBPA/IRF1/KLF4-mediated activation of ECM remodeling and anti-angiogenic programs, with Hofbauer cells directly secreting sFlt-1 contributing to maternal endothelial damage. Late PE is driven by STAT3-mediated local immune dysregulation, with Hofbauer cells exhibiting excessive antigen presentation without systemic anti-angiogenic attack. These represent two mechanistically distinct diseases arising from the same cell of origin through divergent TF wiring.
